# Fair Haven Setback and Dimensional Requirements
## Common Permit Questions

**Decks:** Decks are accessory structures. Deck permit setback requirements follow the accessory structure yard minimums below. A development permit from the Zoning Officer is required. Contact the Construction Official for building permit requirements.

**Sheds:** Sheds are accessory structures subject to the accessory structure setback requirements below.

**Detached garages:** Detached garages are accessory structures subject to the accessory structure setback requirements below.

**Pools:** Swimming pools are subject to Section 11-4 of the Fair Haven Code and accessory structure setbacks.

**Additions:** Home additions follow the principal building setback requirements for your zone.

## Key Notes (from footnotes on source document)

- **(1) B-1 Zone side yards:** Where principal structures on adjacent lots do not comply with the minimum combined side yard required, the distance between the two principal structures must equal the minimum combined side yard required for the zone in question. The larger side yard will be placed to accommodate the location of an existing nonconforming structure on an adjacent lot.
- **(2) B-2 Zone:** Within any B-2 Zone, a building used only for residential purposes shall comply with the requirements of the R-7.5 Zone.
- **(3) Front yard setbacks:** Corner lots shall provide the minimum front yard for the respective zone for both intersecting streets. In residential zones, the "Prevailing Front Yard Setback" may be applicable per Sections 30-2.4 and 30-7.7(f) of the Borough Code. Where the prevailing average setback of properties within 200 feet on the same block face is greater than 20 feet, the minimum setbacks shall match the prevailing setback.
- **(9)(10) Total side yards:** Add 1.5 feet to the total side yard requirement for each full five feet by which the lot width exceeds the minimum lot width for the zone.
- **(12) HA Zone stories:** A minimum roof pitch of 6 on 12 is required.
- **(13) HA Zone front yard:** The minimum setback along Cedar Avenue is 13 ft, increasing to 16 ft at a depth of 55 ft perpendicular to River Road, as shown on the concept plan.
- **(14) HA Zone height:** See note for details.
- **(15) HA Zone FAR:** 0.95 maximum.
- **(16) HA Zone lot requirements:** Corner lot area 28,000 sq ft; width 175 ft along River / 125 ft along Cedar Avenue Road; depth 175 ft.
- **(17) OHA-2 front yard:** 15 ft.
- **(18) OHA rear yards:** Minimum rear yard setback shall be 20 ft.
- **(19) OHA mixed-use principal building height:** 30 ft for mixed-use buildings.
- **(20) OHA mixed-use stories:** 3 stories for mixed-use; 2 stories for residential.
- **(21) OHA-1/OHA-4 front yard:** 15 ft.

## Common Permit Scenarios — Quick Reference

**Deck or patio attached to house (accessory structure):**
Use the **Accessory Buildings and Structures** setback table above. In most residential zones, a deck must be at least 10 ft from the rear property line and at least 5–10 ft from the side property line (depending on zone). Decks attached to the principal dwelling may be treated as part of the principal structure — check with the Zoning Officer.

**Shed or detached garage:**
Use the **Accessory Buildings and Structures** setback table. Maximum accessory building height is 15 ft in all residential zones (R-40 through R-5).

**Home addition:**
Use the **Principal Buildings and Structures** setback table. Building coverage and lot coverage maximums also apply — verify both before designing.

**Most common residential zones in Fair Haven:**
Fair Haven is predominantly R-7.5 (7,500 sq ft minimum lot), R-10 (10,000 sq ft), and R-5 (5,000 sq ft). When a resident does not know their zone, R-7.5 is the most common default for single-family neighborhoods.
